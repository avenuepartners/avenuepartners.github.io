About

Christine Silva partners with corporate and academic clients on research projects, bringing expertise in both quantitative and qualitative research.

Christine was previously a Senior Director in Research at Catalyst, the leading non-profit organization focused on the advancement of women, headquartered in New York. While at Catalyst, she co-authored a longitudinal global study of high-potential employees, with her articles featured in the Harvard Business Review. Christine also launched Catalyst’s first research study on LGBT inclusion and played a lead role in the groundbreaking series on the experiences of visible minorities in corporate Canada.

Taking an evidence-based approach, Christine has worked closely with global companies to leverage insights from research studies in the design and execution of corporate talent management strategy and initiatives. Christine has presented to and consulted with executive leadership teams and corporate diversity councils, as well as non-profit, government, and academic audiences.

Christine received a Master of Industrial Relations from the University of Toronto, a Master of Science in Organizational Behaviour from Queen’s University, and completed doctoral coursework in Organizational Behaviour at Queen’s University.
